What do "Pom" and "Doro" mean?

Pom stands for "apple" (Ethiopian for "ፖም"), representing a healthy focused work session.
Doro is inspired by the Amharic word for "chicken" (ዶሮ), symbolizing a break—just as a chicken takes a break from laying eggs!

Short History of the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. He used a tomato-shaped kitchen timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. This method helps improve focus and productivity by encouraging regular rest and reducing mental fatigue.
